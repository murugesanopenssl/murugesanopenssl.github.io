MIS Saving Strategy

Assume that I have opened MIS sequentially during different months and years as shown below:
  • 01-Jan-2021 – 5000.00 INR – New MIS
  • 02-Mar-2021 – 5000.00 INR – New MIS
  • 03-Jun-2021 – 5000.00 INR – New MIS
  • 04-Sep-2021 – 5000.00 INR – New MIS
  • 05-Dec-2021 – 5000.00 INR – New MIS
  • ...
  • 31-Feb-2023 – 5000.00 INR – New MIS
After completing the 31st day cycle, I again start opening MIS on the 1st, 2nd, 3rd ... up to the 31st during different months and years.

After 5 years, I close the previous MIS online on the exact closing date and open a new MIS again on the same date using the principal amount.

Interest Calculation Examples
  • 2000.0000 at 6.5% interest
    2000 × 6.5 × 1 / 1200 = 10.83333
  • 5000.0000 at 7.4% interest
    5000 × 7.4 × 1 / 1200 ≈ 30.83333
Interest rounding example:
  • If interest is 7.4% → ₹31 for 10 months and ₹30 for 2 months
  • If interest is 6.5% → ₹11 for 10 months and ₹10 for 2 months

  • Medicines

  • Parents' health expenses
From this income, I started saving part of the amount in PPF.

PPF Saving Plan (15 Years)
  1. Minimum deposit per year using Post Office Internet Banking: ₹500
  2. Minimum deposit per year inside Post Office manually: ₹500
  3. Using IPPB Mobile App, deposits allowed in multiples of ₹50
  4. PPF interest is credited every year on 31-Mar
  5. Whenever PPF interest is credited, deposit at least ₹50 using the IPPB mobile app on or before 05-Apr.
  6. I created a PPF account on 13-Oct-2024
  7. I started depositing ₹500 every month
  8. PPF maturity date: 12-Oct-2039
  9. Internet banking deposits: ₹500, ₹600, or ₹500 + multiples of ₹100
  10. IPPB mobile app deposits: ₹50, ₹100, or multiples of ₹50

This saving method indirectly supports India by investing money through government schemes and helps strengthen the country's financial growth.
