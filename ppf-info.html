PPF Nominee and Death Claim Scenario

Assume that:
  • PPF account opening date: Fri 08-Mar-2024
  • PPF maturity date: Thu 31-Mar-2039
  • I am the PPF account holder
  • My wife is registered as nominee through Post Office Internet Banking and the nominee request is approved
  • If I die during the year 2037, before the PPF maturity date
After my death, my wife does not need to wait until Thu 31-Mar-2039. The PPF account will not accept further deposits after my death.

Actions required by my wife after my death:
  1. Obtain my official death certificate from the local authority.
  2. Visit the Post Office branch where my PPF account is maintained.
  3. Submit PPF death claim request using the applicable claim form (such as Form G).
  4. Submit required documents:
    • Death certificate
    • Her identity proof and address proof
    • PPF account number/details available through Post Office records or Internet Banking records
    • Nominee details
    • Her bank account details for receiving payment
  5. Post Office will verify the documents and process the claim.
  6. The eligible PPF balance will be paid to my wife as the registered nominee.
  7. The PPF account will be closed after settlement of the death claim.

Important:
  • My wife cannot continue depositing ₹500 every month or extend my PPF account after my death.
  • My wife does not have to wait for the maturity date Thu 31-Mar-2039.
  • The nominee receives the eligible PPF amount after the claim process is completed.
  • Keeping nominee details updated and approved in Post Office records helps simplify the claim process.

Example:
  • If I die on any date during 2037, my wife can start the claim process after my death.
  • She will receive the PPF amount after verification by the Post Office.
  • The maturity date Thu 31-Mar-2039 will no longer apply because the account is settled due to my death.
MIS Saving Strategy

Assume that I have opened MIS sequentially during different months and years as shown below:

  • 03-Jun-2021 – 5000.00 INR – New MIS
  • 04-Sep-2021 – 5000.00 INR – New MIS
  • 05-Dec-2021 – 5000.00 INR – New MIS
  • ...
  • 31-Feb-2023 – 5000.00 INR – New MIS
After completing the 31st day cycle, I again start opening MIS on the 1st, 2nd, 3rd ... up to the 31st during different months and years.

After 5 years, I close the previous MIS online on the exact closing date and open a new MIS again on the same date using the principal amount.

Interest Calculation Examples
  • 2000.0000 at 6.5% interest
    2000 × 6.5 × 1 / 1200 = 10.83333
  • 5000.0000 at 7.4% interest
    5000 × 7.4 × 1 / 1200 ≈ 30.83333
Interest rounding example:
  • If interest is 7.4% → ₹31 for 10 months and ₹30 for 2 months
  • If interest is 6.5% → ₹11 for 10 months and ₹10 for 2 months
