New Employee Savings Guide

This guide explains how new employees at private companies can manage their salary and savings effectively.

1. Open a Salary Account

  • Open a salary account at SBI (State Bank of India).
  • Reason: SBI is a government bank and provides reliable Internet banking, YONO Lite and YONO SBI mobile apps.
  • We will manage money using banking apps, not by visiting the bank physically.
  • Exception I have seen at a private bank during 2016 IST:
    I went to the private bank at Dharmapuri/Tamilnadu/India during 2016 IST
    Submitted a letter to the private bank manager to close my account.
    Manager told me to give the letter to a near by person.
    Before given my letter I have taken snapshot using my mobile.
    After 3/4 weeks I have received an email informing me to pay 15000.00 INR to that private bank,
    since I have not handled minimum balane at my private bank account from noreply@thatbank.com
    I searched internet and replied that private bank including my snapshot and asked them to view my recorded video inside that bank.
    Next day I have received automated email that my account has been closed successfully.

2. Salary Management

  • Assume your first month salary is ₹21,000.00 .
  • Inform others that you are receiving ₹12,000.00 .
  • Transfer 5,000.00 ₹/INR from your bank (using NEFT without using IMPS) to your Post Office account Internet banking.

3. Monthly Deposits

  • After receiving your salary, transfer the last month last balance from your salary account to your Post Office account using NEFT without using IMPS.

4. Opening MIS

  • Whenever your saved amount at post-office reaches 5,000.00 ₹/INR, open a MIS (Monthly Income Scheme) account at the Post Office using internet banking without travelling and wasting petrol/diesel.
  • Open MIS sequentially on different days (1st, 2nd, 3rd … 31st) of different months or years.
  • Example: 01-Jan-2026, 02-Mar-2026, 03-Jun-2026, …
  • Reason for using 5,000.00:
    a) This principal used to give 31.00 interest during 10 months and 30.00 during 2 months.
    Like the same we receive interest every month for 5 years.
    After five years, re-deposit your (original post office MIS) principal to MIS again.
    However after five years, use principal based on current MIS interest at post office. Like: 5000.00 if MIS interest is 7.4%...

5. MIS Interest

  • Since each month has 4 or 5 Sundays, MIS interest (₹31.00 or 30.00) will be credited to your post office savings account on Saturdays instead of Sundays.
  • Assume that christmas/ramzan/Deepavali/... public holidays came on Monday.
    MIS interest on monday will be received on sunday.
    Due to sunday we will receive interest on Saturday.
  • Save MIS interest in Post Office itself.
  • Once the saved amount reaches 5,000.00 ₹/INR again, open a new MIS using Internet banking without physically visiting the post office, saving fuel and reducing pollution.
