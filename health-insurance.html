As an Indian citizen I want to share my thoughts about health insurance based on a real life experience.
One of my close friends met with a serious accident on Wed 14-Dec-2005 IST.
He was in a coma from 14-Dec-2005 to Sun 15-Jan-2006 IST.
After he returned home on Sun 15-Jan-2006 IST, he slowly started recovery.
On 06-Feb-2006 at about 06:30 PM IST I explained to him the full story of the accident.
He only remembered getting a seat inside a bus while travelling to Chennai on 13-Dec-2005.
His first lawyer passed away in 2013 IST.
A second lawyer completed the case in 2016 IST at Dharmapuri, Tamil Nadu, India.
Because of the accident my friend permanently lost the vision in his left eye.
The accident insurance compensation was finally settled in 2016 IST.
The lawyer suggested shifting the case to Chennai to claim more money, but my friend decided to finish the case locally.
After this experience I suggested another way for him and his family to save money for medical emergencies.
Example of a typical health insurance payment.
Assume a person pays
10000.00 ₹ INR every month for one year at (private or SBI) insurance provider
from Tue 01-Apr-2025 IST to Tue 31-Mar-2026 IST.
Total payment in one year:
12 × 10000.00 = 120000.00 (One lakh twenty thousand rupees).
In the next year a new insurance policy must be started again.
If there is no hospitalization, the only benefit may be income tax reduction.
The money paid is not saved for future medical needs.
If the same payment continues for 10 years:
120000.00 × 10 = 1200000.00
Twelve lakh rupees paid to insurance companies.
Another idea is to save money using government postal savings schemes
such as the Post Office Monthly Income Scheme (MIS).
For example a person may deposit 5000.00 ₹ INR MIS on different dates such as the 1st, 2nd, up to the 31st across different months and years.
MIS accounts can usually be pre-closed after 3 years if money is needed for emergencies such as hospitalization.
Example values:
If 3000.00 ₹ INR MIS is pre-closed after 4 years, about 2940.00 ₹ INR may be received.
If 5000.00 ₹ INR MIS is pre-closed after 3 years, about 4900.00 ₹ INR may be received.
If 5000.00 ₹ INR MIS is pre-closed after 4 years, about 4867.00 ₹ INR may be received.
Because of this idea I personally feel that opening small MIS deposits and
pre-closing them only during emergencies can keep money available when needed.
Even when MIS is pre-closed,
the money remains within the national financial system and
indirectly supports the country's economy.
These are my personal thoughts shared as an Indian citizen.
Citizens of other countries may also follow similar ideas
depending on the postal savings or government savings systems
available in their country.
