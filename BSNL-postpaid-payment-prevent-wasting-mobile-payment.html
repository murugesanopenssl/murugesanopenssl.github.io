BSNL Postpaid Payment - Prevent Wasting Mobile Payment Charges
I noticed that paying my BSNL postpaid bill directly from the Google Pay mobile application may add a platform fee.
To avoid unnecessary charges,
I always pay through the BSNL customer portal using SBI e-PAY and then scan the displayed QR code with the Google Pay mobile application.

Recommended Payment Method Amount Paid
1. Open the BSNL customer self-care portal https://wsc.cdr.bsnl.co.in/index
2. Log in.
3. Select the required BSNL broadband or fibre connection.
4. Choose SBI e-PAY.
5. Scan the displayed QR code using the Google Pay mobile application.
6. Complete the payment. 		Scan the QR code from browser using gpay at mobile app.
I have paid exactly 470.00 inr and 570.00 INR without any platform fee/sercive charges from gpay.
Only the actual bill amount is paid.
No Google Pay platform fee was added in my experience when using https://wsc.cdr.bsnl.co.in/index for payment.
Connection Observed Payment
Bangalore BSNL Fibernet 530.00 INR using SBI e-PAY QR code.
Dharmapuri BSNL Fibernet 470.00 INR using SBI e-PAY QR code.
My Recommendation
Avoid paying the BSNL postpaid bill directly from the Google Pay bill reminder if it displays a platform fee.

Instead, log in to the BSNL customer portal, select SBI e-PAY, scan the QR code with the Google Pay mobile application, and pay only the actual bill amount.
