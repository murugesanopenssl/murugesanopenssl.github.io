ALWAYS HAVE BANK BALANCE ENDING WITH ZERO

On Tue 14-Jul-2026 IST I went to BIG-MART shop at Attibele/Bengaluru/Karnataka/India.

BIG-MART shop accepts SBI ATM card for payment.

HOW SHOP POINTS ARE CREATED

When I buy any item from a shop, the printed MRP amount is paid to the shop.

Example:

Item MRP: 200.00 INR

The shop accepts 200.00 INR as payment.

From the accepted amount, a small amount (example: 0.50 INR) is converted into points and added to my points balance inside the shop's computer application.

This is how customers receive and save points inside the shop's computer application.

PAYMENT CALCULATION

My SBI bank balance was:

1000.79 INR

After buying items at that shop, I needed to pay:

206.50 INR

My points available inside that shop's computer application were:

20.00 INR

I used a calculator and calculated:

206.50 - 0.79 = 205.71

Hence, I requested the shop to deduct 0.71 INR from my available points.

After point deduction:

206.50 - 0.71 = 205.79

I paid 205.79 INR using my SBI ATM card.

After the transaction:

1000.79 - 205.79 = 795.00

RESULT

My bank balance became 795.00 INR, which ends with .00.

Keeping the bank balance ending with zero helps maintain a simple and easy-to-remember account balance.
