SBI Fixed Deposit – Short-Term Interest Optimization

1. My Question to ChatGPT

Date/Time: Fri 09-Jan-2026 09:33 AM IST

Today fixed deposit closing: 28,016.00 (principal)ரூ/₹/INR, total 28,032.00 (interest 16.00 ரூ/₹/INR).
On Tue 13-Jan-2026 IST: I am receiving ETDR principal of 1,042.00 ரூ/₹/INR, and total amount of 1,045.00 (principal 1,042.00 ரூ/₹/INR + interest 3.00 ரூ/₹/INR).
I need to pay BSNL 1,020.00 ரூ/₹/INR on or before Mon 19-Jan-2026 IST.

I am planning to pay the BSNL amount on Tue 13-Jan-2026 IST from the closing fixed deposit of 1,045.00 ரூ/₹/INR.
Today, I can take 1,020.00 ரூ/₹/INR from today's ETDR amount 28,032.00 ரூ/₹/INR and keep 27,012.00 ரூ/₹/INR as principal to open new fixed deposit again for 7 day ETDR till end of this month 31-Jan-2026 using 7-day terms.
Which is better to earn more ETDR interest?

  • Option 1: Take 1,020.00 ரூ/₹/INR from today’s closed principal, re-open 27,012.00 ரூ/₹/INR as fixed deposit, and also use 1,045.00 ரூ/₹/INR as principal on Tue 13-Jan-2026 IST repeatedly till 31-Jan-2026 for 7-day terms.
  • Option 2: Take 1,045.00 ரூ/₹/INR on Tue 13-Jan-2026 IST and pay BSNL on Tue 13-Jan-2026 IST.

2. Initial Response from ChatGPT

The initial analysis suggested paying the bill later would be better, based on the general principle that keeping a larger principal invested for a longer period usually earns more interest.
Hence pay on Tue 13-Jan-2026 IST.

Limitation: This did not account for SBI’s rounding-to-the-nearest-rupee method applied to short-term fixed deposits.

3. Knowledge Transfer to ChatGPT

Date/Time: Fri 09-Jan-2026 09:45 AM IST

SBI interest calculation rule clarified with real values:

SBI ETDR INTEREST = ROUND(PRINCIPAL × 3.05 × 7 / 36500 , 0)
If interest having greater than or equal to 0.50 round that to 1.00 ரூ/₹/INR
If interest having less than 0.50 round that to 0.00 ரூ/₹/INR
  • Interest rate: 3.05% per annum
  • Tenure: 7 days
  • Interest rounded to the nearest rupee

Key insights transferred:

  • SBI rounding causes interest to be earned in discrete steps
  • Splitting deposits can increase total interest
  • Early payment can sometimes improve overall returns
This knowledge corrected the earlier assumption and led to the conclusion that paying the bill earlier and splitting deposits earned more interest.
