I have started the following steps from 2023 until now to increase my daily, weekly, and monthly savings.
STEP 01

My mother had a fixed deposit at Indian Bank for 10 years. The principal amount was ₹400,000, and she used to receive ₹2,147 each month as interest.

On 10-Dec-2025 (IST), I went to Indian Bank and closed this fixed deposit.

Reasons for closing:
1) She received interest every month on the 10th.
2) The deposit renewal date each year was 10-Dec.

After closing the FD:
– I transferred the entire ₹400,000 from her Indian Bank account to my SBI account using NEFT.
– Then from my SBI account, I transferred ₹400,000 to my mother’s Post Office account using NEFT.

On Fri, 12-Dec-2025, I opened a Monthly Income Scheme (MIS) in my mother’s Post Office account through internet banking.

She will receive:
– ₹2,473 for 10 months
– ₹2,472 for 2 months

From Jan-2026, I will transfer ₹2,300 to my mother every month (on the 12th) for her expenses.
The remaining amount (2472 − 2300 = ₹172) will be saved in her Post Office account for 26 months (2 years 6 months).

STEP 02

I need to pay my son’s school fees of ₹55,000 on Sat, 10-Jan-2026.

To prepare, I opened a fixed deposit of ₹27,968 at SBI on 10-Dec-2025.
This FD will close on 10-Jan-2026.

For every fixed deposit at SBI, I always submit Form 15G for income tax purposes.
STEP 03

Whenever my mother’s Post Office account savings reach ₹1,000, I transfer it to my SBI account and open a 46-day fixed deposit.
STEP 04

I have started saving money in my joint Post Office account for my son’s school fees.
Whenever the savings reach ₹1,000, I open a fixed deposit at SBI for 46 days or 7 days, depending on the school fee due date.

Although I open multiple fixed deposits, I always ensure each FD has a **different closing date**.
Reason: To avoid service charges.
STEP 05

Whenever I save ₹5,000, I open an MIS at the Post Office on any day of the month:
01–AnyMonth–AnyYear
02–AnyMonth–AnyYear
...
31–AnyMonth–AnyYear

Reason:
By opening MIS accounts on different dates, I receive MIS interest on different days, which helps me manage my daily spending.
