I prefer all users on Earth to recharge their mobile one day after the SIM expiry date.

All banks in this world provide interest to customers once every 3 months.
Hence, they provide interest 4 times per year.

Assume that a bank is going to provide interest to customers on Fri 25-Dec-2026.

Assume that I have 1000.00 INR in my bank account on 01-Oct-2026.
From 01-Oct-2026 to 25-Dec-2026, I have not withdrawn or deposited any money.

The bank calculates the total amount for these days:

TOTAL AMB SUM = 1000.00 × 86 days
TOTAL AMB SUM = 86,000.00

For this AMB amount, the bank provides some interest on 25-Dec-2026.

If I recharge my SIM exactly on the expiry date, my total balance will reduce on that day.
If I recharge one day after the expiry date, my total balance reduces one day later.

Hence, my AMB interest may be slightly affected if I recharge exactly on expiry date.

I have been following this practice for my own SIM from 2003 until now,
for my mother from 2005 until now,
and for my father from 2024 until now.

a) Never recharge a SIM on the expiry date.
b) Always recharge any SIM for one year instead of 28 / 84 / 90 days to prevent wastage of money.
