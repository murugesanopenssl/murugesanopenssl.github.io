💡 Practical Scenario & Operational Frequency:

Assume that a person opens a Post Office Monthly Income Scheme (MIS) account with an amount of 5000.00 sequentially across the 1st, 2nd, ... up to the 31st day of different months and across different years. Upon completing a 31-day cycle, this exact pattern is repeated systematically again and again for many consecutive years.

⚠️ Impact of Financial Interruptions (Job Loss / Business Downturn):

In the event of unexpected job loss or a sudden change in business conditions, that person relies on receiving daily MIS interest streams to directly manage critical day-to-day survival overheads (such as daily spending, food, traveling, school fees, utility bills, and donations).

🚫 Critical Limitation: This precise daily cash flow requirement cannot be achieved when utilizing a standard TERM DEPOSIT—even though a Term Deposit mathematically provides higher cumulative interest values than an MIS—because that peak yield is locked and only fully realized after 5 years.
