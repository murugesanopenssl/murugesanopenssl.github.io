Thoughts on Insurance and Savings I want to share my thoughts with people worldwide, especially lower and middle-class families. On Insurance Many people pay insurance premiums (e.g., Rs. 10,000 per month for a year, totaling Rs. 1,20,000) to private or SBI insurance companies. If no hospital expenses occur during the year, this money does not create savings for the family. Over 10 years, this approach can result in Rs. 12,00,000 spent with no tangible savings. Insurance may only reduce taxable income, but it does not directly save money for emergencies or parental care. Alternative: Monthly Installment Plan (MIS) with India Post Instead of spending on insurance, consider investing in a post office Monthly Installment Plan (MIS): Open MIS accounts monthly or at different times in the year. You can close MIS accounts prematurely after a few years if needed. Example: Closing a Rs. 3,000 MIS after 3 years may return Rs. 4,900; after 4 years, Rs. 2,940. MIS can provide indirect income that remains in India and can be reinvested. Example of My MIS Strategy I opened 47 MIS accounts, each with a principal of Rs. 3,000. After prematurely closing 39 MIS accounts after 4 years, I received Rs. 2,940 per account: 39 × 2,940 = Rs. 1,14,660 This amount was used to pay my children’s Cambridge IGCSE exam fees (Rs. 1,12,000). Remaining Rs. 2,660 stayed in my post office account. Strategy for MIS Interest Usage: Meet daily expenses and medicine costs. Save for parents’ insurance; once Rs. 1,000 is saved, open an ETDR account in SBI for 46 days. Submit Form 15G online to avoid unnecessary travel and pollution. Save to open a new MIS account once Rs. 5,000 is reached. Save for next year’s school fees for children. Save for future purchases, e.g., a printer. Save for children’s school trips.
